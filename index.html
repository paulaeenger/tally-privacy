Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 7, 2026

Tally is a read-only personal-finance app for tracking your household's accounts, transactions, budgets, and goals. Tally can view your financial data but never moves money, makes payments, or transfers funds. This policy explains what data Tally handles and why. Tally does not sell your data, does not use it for advertising, and does not track you across other apps or websites.

What we collect

  • Account information: your name, email address, and a unique user ID, used to sign you in and identify your account (via email/password or Google Sign-In).
  • Financial information: account balances and transactions retrieved from financial institutions you choose to connect (through SimpleFIN), plus any accounts or transactions you enter manually.
  • Content you create: categories, budgets, goals, and notes you add.

How it's used and stored

Your data is used only to provide the app's features, showing you your finances. It is stored in our backend database (Supabase) and scoped to your household, so it is visible only to members of your household. The read-only credential used to fetch your bank data is stored encrypted and is never stored on your device.

Third-party services

  • Supabase: authentication and data storage.
  • SimpleFIN: read-only aggregation of your connected bank accounts.
  • Google Sign-In: optional sign-in.

These providers process data only to deliver their part of the service. We do not share your data with anyone for marketing.

Your choices

You can disconnect accounts, delete transactions and accounts, and request deletion of your account and associated data at any time by contacting us.
